Sewing


Although I have been sewing since childhood, my prime focus nowadays is cosplay, a hobby I took up in 2011. I have created quite the collection over the years, but I have made a selection of the costumes I am most proud of, which you can find below.

2B

NieR Automata was a game that took my heart and crushed it in all the right ways, so when my best friend asked me if I would cosplay 2B with her as 9S, I immediately agreed.

We wanted to make the costumes to a high standard, and wanted to enter them into qualifiers for international competitions. In the end, the cosplays took almost two years to make, and were finally finished in 2019. Since I was the most experienced, I was responsible for transforming the nonsensical game designs into real-life patterns, and I helped with the more difficult parts of 9S’s costume, like setting in the sleeves.

The results were worth it, and we ended up placing runner-up in the Belgian preselections for C4. My favourite part of the cosplay is the massive sword (it’s over 2 metres big!) and the 12 metres of hand embroidery that went into the skirt.

Mikaela Hyakuya

This is the first costume I made without the guidance of my grandmother, who taught me almost everything I know. As such, it was a culmination of all I had learned. I finished the cosplay in 2016. It went on to win a few smaller contests and became runner-up for the Belgian preselections of Eurocosplay 2017. In 2019, it made a trip to the MAS (Museum aan de Stroom) in Antwerp, where it was part of their ‘Cool Japan’ exhibition.

Despite its age and history, the cosplay is still in good shape and I still enjoy wearing it because of its combination of comfort and elegance.

Mako Mankanshoku

Mako Manknashoku was one of my earlier, more ‘complex’ cosplays. It was made in 2014, and if I were to do it all over again, I’d do it very differently, with a bigger focus on wearability. The base uniform is made entirely out of polyester, and there is nothing keeping the jacket from falling off the shoulders, resulting in a costume with low breathability and restricted movement.

Still, I am proud of the way it looks and with what I was able to create with my limited experience at the time. I especially enjoyed hand painting the inside of the coat, and patterning the shirt collar just so that it looks like a pair of eyes, if you squint a little.

Thanatos - Work in Progress

Even though I started this cosplay as a ‘quick project’, it has been in progress for a very, very long time already. The patterning turned out to be a lot harder than I had imagined, and other, less exciting but more necessary projects (read: everyday clothes) keep getting priority.

Still, I wanted to highlight it here, because I am very proud of the project. I really love the drape of the mock-up, which was a new process for me and took a lot of iterations. But my favourite part, without a doubt, is the goldwork brooch.
